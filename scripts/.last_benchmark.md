# Single-core decode benchmark — 172432 messages from long_flight.csv

Procedure mirrors `jet1090/python/examples/benchmark.py`: pandas-loaded
corpus, `data.rawmsg.str[18:]` iteration, 7 runs of 1 loop each,
mean ± stdev. Only single-core paths measured (rs1090 invoked with
`batch=n` to force single-core).

Corpus: `/home/junzi/arc/code/4-fork/jet1090/crates/rs1090/data/long_flight.csv`
pymodes version: `3.0.0.dev0`

| Decoder | Wall time (mean) | Throughput | vs pymodes v3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| rs1090 (single-core) | 5.59s ± 0.01 | 30,839 msg/s | 0.43× |
| pyModeS 2.21.1 (c_common, C) | 4.87s ± 0.02 | 35,416 msg/s | 0.49× |
| pyModeS 2.21.1 (py_common, pure Python) | 8.99s ± 0.02 | 19,170 msg/s | 0.27× |
| **pymodes v3 (pure Python)** | **2.39s ± 0.01** | **72,201 msg/s** | **1.00×** |

**Headline:** pymodes v3 is 3.77× faster than pyModeS 2.21.1 py_common, 2.04× vs pyModeS 2.21.1 c_common (compiled C), and 2.34× vs rs1090 single-core Rust.
